Authfluent FAQ

How does Authfluent store my data?

Authfluent runs entirely in your web browser. Your secret keys and 2FA tokens are stored strictly on your local device within the browser's local storage. No data is ever sent to an external server or a remote database.

What happens if I clear my browser cache or data?

Because Authfluent relies entirely on local browser storage to keep your data private, completely wiping your browser data, clearing local storage, or using certain aggressive cache-cleaning extensions will remove your saved accounts. Generally, cache-cleaning extensions will not remove your accounts, but it is still recommended to use any whitelist feature on the extension if it has one.

Why is Authfluent completely free?

Authfluent Co. was built to counter predatory modern authenticator apps that lock basic utility behind paywalls, impose restrictive three-account limits, or require subscription fees. Our tool provides full, unrestricted 2FA code generation with zero upgrades or fees.

How are 2FA codes generated?

The app processes standard authentication algorithms locally in the browser using the secret keys you enter or scan via QR codes, displaying the changing time-based tokens entirely on the client side.
